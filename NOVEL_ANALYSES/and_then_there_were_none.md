This study aims to analyze the methodology employed by the killer in selecting their victims, examining the inherent relationship between the characters' pasts and their criminal psychology. The novel draws critical attention not merely to the commission of a single crime, but rather to the complex psychology and defensive behaviors of its many characters. As early as Chapter 1, the individuals, as a general rule, utilize Projection to deflect attention towards the superficial flaws of others in an attempt to conceal their own defects. This mechanism serves as a process of self-justification rooted in criminal psychology.
